About Us

The key objective of Seenage Life is to provide a platform for senior citizens to find a way back into the workforce. We believe that age does not limit ability, performance or skill and that seniors (seenagers) have a lot to contribute to society, as regards experience, advice and guidance.

What We Do

Our goal is to ensure that seenagers continue their professional involvement, have a sense of purpose and achievement while earning their living. Even though seenagers are experienced they tend to be an under-rated resource and employment opportunities for them are far and few.

Jobs for You

We are actively engaged with NGOs & corporates, helping seenagers find work either full time, part time, paid or voluntary. Seenage Academy is being set up so that seenagers can upskill themselves in their areas of interest and / or passion, enabling them to secure employment.

Our Community

Seenage Life also provides a platform for Seenagers to Volunteer for tutoring, mentoring or counselling, Share articles and events happening in and around their city and Conduct events.

Ask Experts

We partner with well-known experts across various functions relevant to the community. As members you can ask questions and receive answers in complete confidentiality from our experts.

Products that help

We present a curated set of useful products with every single best option available. Just pick what you want based on your need and get them delivered home, from reputed online markets. It’s that easy! You can also rate and review products on this platform after using them, to help others make better choices. Purchase and product delivery will be through reliable external partner sites.

Who We Are

This website is being run as a sustainable enterprise by its promoters, Seenage Life and was originally initiated by Tata Trust and managed by SocialAlpha. Our community includes seniors and experts in the senior space and a small group of evangelists to manage the website. Core to the community are our seenagers - they help decide what’s interesting and what is not for the community.
